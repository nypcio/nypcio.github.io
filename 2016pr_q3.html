Turn on the Lights!

In a 2D lattice grid space, there are mirrors that are slanted in a 45 degree angle or 135 degree angle, and a lamp.
We want to shoot lasers that are parallel to either the row or the column of the grid from the outside.
If the laser hits the lamp, the lamp turns on. If the lamp hits the mirror, depending on the direction of the mirror, the laser takes a turn of 90 degrees.

Find one way to shoot the laser in order to turn on the lamp.

Input

First line of input will have the width W and height H of the 2D lattice grid space. (W, H are both integers greater than or equal to 1 and less than or equal to 30.)

A W by H lattice grid will be given in these characters :
- . : empty space
- / : 45 degree slanted mirror
- \ : 135 degree slanted mirror
- O : lamp

Output

Output one possible way of turning on the map.

Depending on the direction of the laser, output the result as follows:
- If shot from the left side of the grid: L (number of rows down from the top)
- If shot from the right side of the grid: R (number of rows down from the top)
- If shot from the top of the grid: U (number of columns from the left)
- If shot from the bottom of the grid: D (number of columns from the left)

The very top and the very left grid counts as the 0th row/column.

Input Example

       5 4
       .....
       /.\..
       /.O..
       ..\..

Output Example

 L 2

To turn the lamp on from the input grid, there are four possible answers: L 2, R 2, D 0, R 3.
You will get full marks for outputting any one of the possible answers.
