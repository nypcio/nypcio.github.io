Blue Area

On a white canvas (2-D), we draw and color in a square where the sides are parallel with the x and y axes of the 2-D coordinate system with blue markers. As a result, the frame and the inside of the square will be all colored blue.
N of these squares lie on a plane. Each square will be represented by its bottom left coordinate and the length of the square. In such a case, our goal is to write a program on how many of these blue areas can occur.
Here, the connected blue area means that if arbitrary points a and b were selected, an ant can started at point a and get to point b by solely crossing the blue area (by at least one route). By using the fact that the sides of the square are parallel to the x and y axes, we can redefine the blue area P as: for two arbitrary points a and b in P, by connecting a and b and with lines parallel to the axes in the blue area gives us at least one route.

In the figure below, we see 3 square with 2 squares with side length 1 with the bottom left coordinates being (1, 1) and (2, 2). We also see one with side length 2 with the bottom left coordinate being (4, 1).
Let us label the left 2 squares A and B. A and B overlap at point (2, 2) and because the square area includes the frame, these 2 squares form one blue area. In other words, if we choose an arbitary point a in A and a point b in B, with a combination of lines parallel to the x and y axes, a path P can be made that connects point a and point b within that blue area. This path must pass (2, 2). In this diagram, we see that there are 2 connected blue areas.

Input

The first line has the number N, which represents the number of squares and N can be greater than or equal to 1 and less than or equal to 1000.
Then, the next N lines have 3 values a, b, and L separated by a space. (0 ≥ a, b ≤ 109). a, b here represent the x and y coordinate of the bottom left coordinate of the square and L is the square's side length. Every square's coordinates are less than 10 9.

Output

Output the number of blue areas.

Input Example 1

       3
       1 1 1 
       2 2 1
       4 1 1

Output Example 1

        2

Input Example 2

       5
       1 1 2
       2 2 2
       4 1 1 
       5 2 1 
       5 2 1

Output Example 2

        1

Marks Guideline

          - Type 1 (25 marks): N ≤ 100, and all x and y coordinates of the points of the square should be less than or equal to 100.
          - Type 2 (60 marks): N ≤ 1000, and all x and y coordinates of the points of the square should be less than or equal to 1000.
          - Type 3 (35 marks): No other restrictions other than the ones listed in the problem.
